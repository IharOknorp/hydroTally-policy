# Privacy Policy for HydroTally

Last updated: 2026-04-10

This Privacy Policy explains how HydroTally collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the HydroTally mobile application and its widget.

## 1. Who operates HydroTally

HydroTally is operated by:

- `Ihar Pronka`
- Contact email: `oknorpcorp@gmail.com`
- Jurisdiction: `Poland`

## 2. What information the app processes

### 2.1 Information you enter in the app

HydroTally processes information that you choose to enter or generate while using the app, including:

- hydration logs, such as date, time, amount, beverage type, and container preset
- daily goal settings and measurement preferences
- reminder preferences
- quick log preferences, preset selections, and language selection

### 2.2 Information stored locally on your device

HydroTally stores most app data locally on your device. This includes:

- your hydration history
- app settings
- current quick-log context
- local premium status used by the app UI

### 2.3 Widget and shared app data

If you use the HydroTally widget, the app stores a limited hydration snapshot in an App Group container shared between the app and the widget so the widget can display current progress.

### 2.4 Notifications

If you enable reminders, HydroTally requests permission to send local notifications. Reminder schedules are created on your device. HydroTally does not use a remote push notification server for reminders in the current app setup.

### 2.5 Purchases

HydroTally offers a paid option to remove banner ads. In-app purchase handling may involve:

- Apple App Store payment and receipt systems
- RevenueCat, which is used to manage purchase state and restoration when configured

HydroTally itself does not process your payment card details. Payment information is handled by Apple.

### 2.6 Advertising

The free version of HydroTally displays banner ads using Google AdMob. Google may collect or receive information related to ad delivery, fraud prevention, diagnostics, and measurement, which may include:

- device and app identifiers
- IP address or network-derived information
- app interaction and ad interaction information
- basic diagnostic information

Google acts under its own privacy terms for the ad services it provides. See:

- [Google AdMob privacy guidance](https://developers.google.com/admob/ios/privacy)
- [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)

### 2.7 Information HydroTally does not currently request from you

Based on the current app implementation, HydroTally does not request or intentionally collect:

- account registration data
- precise location
- contacts
- photos
- camera data
- microphone data
- HealthKit data

## 3. How HydroTally uses information

HydroTally uses information to:

- display hydration progress and history
- power the app widget
- schedule local reminders
- support premium purchase restoration
- show banner ads in the free version
- maintain app functionality, preferences, and language settings

HydroTally is a utility app and is not intended to provide medical advice.

## 4. Legal bases for EEA and UK users

If you are in the EEA or the UK, HydroTally generally relies on one or more of the following legal bases:

- performance of a contract, to provide the app features you request
- legitimate interests, to operate, secure, and improve the app
- consent, where required for certain notifications, ad-related processing, or similar optional features

## 5. Sharing of information

HydroTally may share information with service providers only to the extent needed to operate the app:

- Apple, for App Store purchases and related platform services
- RevenueCat, for purchase management and restore flows when configured
- Google AdMob, for advertising services in the free version

HydroTally does not currently include a user account backend or cloud sync service for hydration history.

## 6. Data retention

HydroTally generally keeps app data on your device until:

- you delete the data in the app
- you uninstall the app
- the data is replaced by newer local app data

Third-party providers such as Apple, RevenueCat, and Google may retain data under their own retention policies.

## 7. Your choices

You can:

- edit or delete hydration entries in the app, where those controls are available
- turn reminders on or off
- purchase the premium version to remove banner ads
- restore eligible purchases through the app
- uninstall the app to remove locally stored app data from your device
